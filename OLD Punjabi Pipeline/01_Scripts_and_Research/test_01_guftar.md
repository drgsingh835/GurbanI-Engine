## 📚 Linguistic Research: "Guftar" (گفتار / ਗੁਫ਼ਤਾਰ)

### 1. Etymology (The Roots)
- **Origin**: Persian (Farsi).
- **Root Verb**: *Goftan* (to speak / to say).
- **Sanskrit Cognates**: Related to the root *Gup* (to speak/protect), though the direct path is through Old Persian *gaub-* (to speak).
- **Nuance**: In Punjabi, "Guftar" isn't just "talking"; it implies **eloquence, discourse, or the manner of speech.** It is a higher-register word than "Gall-baat."

### 2. Academic Context
- **Usage**: Used in formal literature, political discourse, and high-level social etiquette.
- **Related Terms**: 
    - *Guftagu* (Conversation/Dialogue).
    - *Khush-Guftar* (One who speaks beautifully/eloquently).
